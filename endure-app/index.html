Brand Selection Criteria
Every brand on ENDURE passes a rigorous 7-pillar evaluation. Brands are re-assessed annually. Only the top 15% earn listing.
🧵
1. Material Quality Threshold
Fabrics must meet minimum standards for fiber integrity, tensile strength, and pilling resistance.
Minimum 300g/m² for outerwear fabrics
Natural fiber content ≥60% OR proven high-performance synthetics
No fast-fashion blends (polyester >50% without performance justification)
Colorfastness rating Grade 4+ (ISO 105 standard)
🔨
2. Construction Standards
Garments must use construction methods proven to outlast standard sewing techniques.
Double or triple stitching at stress points
Bar-tacking at pocket corners and belt loops
YKK or equivalent quality zippers only
Seam strength ≥200N (EN ISO 13935 test)
📅
3. Expected Lifespan
Products must demonstrate a verified usable lifespan above the category average.
Outerwear: 7+ years expected lifespan
Denim: 5+ years with regular wear
Footwear: resoleable OR 10+ year construction
Verified by owner surveys (n > 500)
⚖️
4. Price-to-Value Ratio
We calculate Cost-Per-Wear (CPW) adjusted for repairability and resale value.
CPW must beat category median by 30%+
Repairability adds to value score
Resale value retention tracked (3-year avg)
Luxury brands must justify premium via CPW
🔧
5. Repairability & Brand Support
Long-term wearability depends on whether a brand supports its products post-purchase.
Offers repair service OR freely available parts
Replacement zippers, buttons, patches available
No intentional product obsolescence
🔬
6. Transparency & Traceability
Brands must disclose where and how products are made.
Published factory or supplier list
Clear material composition on all products
No misleading eco/durability claims
📊
7. Community Validation
Real user longevity data supplements our editorial assessments.
Minimum 4.0/5 long-term owner rating
Less than 15% report major defects within 3 years
Active presence in quality-focused communities
